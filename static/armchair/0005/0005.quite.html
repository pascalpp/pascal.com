Actually, nobody is happy. You get in a car, you can pretty much prepare yourself for some unhappiness. I haven't driven in a long time, but I remember the feeling of frustration each time a light turned red, the reluctance to use the brakes unless absolutely necessary. I hated the stress of rush hour traffic. I wondered why the hell so many other people had to be driving at the same time.

And yet, have you ever seen a car commercial which portrays the hell of 5 o'clock gridlock? No sir, it's all open road and mountain passes, if you buy this car.

I remember hating pedestrians, what the hell are they doing, trying to cross the street? Can't they see I'm in a car and I can go faster than them and I am therefore more important than them?

The sickest part of all is the loss of human scale in our daily lives. There is no longer a definition of space that is centered on the human, there is only the definition of space that is centered on the human who happens to be encased in a car. Oh, there is simply too much to cover here. I'll talk about zoning and urban planning some other time.
