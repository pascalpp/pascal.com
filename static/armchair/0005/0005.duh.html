  So then it was the fifties. The GIs were home, they had the fat wallet for crushing the Krauts, they weren't putting their kids on no bus, no sir. They was buying the fat car and the fat house and fucking the fat wife. (Hey, I'm sorry, I get cranky when I think about the fifties.) This was when America's love affair with the car really took off.

The highway lobby was born. People wanted roads, they wanted to go anywhere, there was money in the bank and gas in the tank, so why shouldn't they be able to go anywhere and everywhere? So the big companies hired the big lobbyists with the big guns to talk the big talk in the big dome in the big D.C. And the roads were built.
