  hmm, where do i fucking begin?

i saw this documentary on PBS (boy what a cheesy beginning, oh well, so much for mass appeal) called 'Taken For A Ride'. It was about the so-called 'modernization' process that was advocated in many cities in the early 20th century, whereby clean, efficient, widely-used, and even profitable trolley-based mass transit systems were slowly, or sometimes not-so-slowly, phased out in favor of buses.

The documentary then goes on to explain how the buses were less popular, convincing people to 'invest in their own individuality' by purchasing a car. The company that ran the bus line (usually National City Bus Lines, a company funded by General Motors, whose motto was 'What's good for GM is good for America') then proclaimed that the bus system was not profitable and cut service, making it less popular.

Duh.
