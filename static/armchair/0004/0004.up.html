well, i been feeling like i'm wasting my time doing my own website. here's the deal:

i have a pretty cynical opinion about the average american. i imagine them in minivans, shopping at wal-mart, watching married with children, getting fat. this disposition contains elements of self-hatred, for these are all things that i at one time or another have taken part in. i find it disturbing how people find a commonality in watching the same tv shows. they have a few moments to talk at the office or whatever, and they 'gossip' about whatever happened on friends or seinfeld the night before. their only commonality is a vicarious desire to live in these 30-minute psychodramas. they rarely actually _do_ anything together. except maybe watch tv.

see, pretty cynical, eh?

i know that things aren't really that bad; i'm sort of casting it in a high-contrast light. anyway, i'm starting to feel that the web is the same schmooze in a lot of ways. take the couch potato off the couch, put it in an office chair, shove it right up next to the screen, and wean it off the remote by giving it a mouse. so it's a little more interactive, so they get to actually choose their destiny, how different is it really?

 
  armchair #0004 - Wednesday, July 10, 1996
